Privacy Policy

Thammasat University Hospital (‚ÄúHospital‚Äù or ‚Äúwe‚Äù) respects your privacy and protects personal data related to the use of the TUH MEWS (Modified Early Warning Score) application for patient monitoring.

This policy clarifies our processes for collecting, using, disclosing, and/or transferring personal data in accordance with the Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (2019) ("PDPA").

1. Scope of This Policy

This policy covers all operations related to personal data collected through TUH MEWS by hospital personnel, including patient data used for condition monitoring and medical service improvement.

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Data of Application Users (Staff)

  • Full Name
  • Nurse ID (or Staff ID)

2.2 Patient Data

  • Full Name
  • Age
  • Bed Number
  • Hospital Number (Patient ID)
  • Ward
  • MEWS score history

3. How We Collect Data

  • Data Received Directly from Staff: Staff information is collected upon registration or assignment.
  • Data Recorded by Personnel: Patient information and vital signs recorded for MEWS assessment.

4. Purposes of Data Processing

  • Monitor and assess patient health conditions continuously.
  • Allow medical personnel to diagnose emergency conditions promptly.
  • Store MEWS score history.
  • Academic research (subject to approval by the Ethics Committee).
  • Analyze data to improve medical service guidelines.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

  • Public interest in public health (PDPA Sections 24(4) and 26(1)).
  • Research or statistical purposes with safeguards.
  • Public health benefits or social protection.
  • Compliance with medical welfare laws.
  • Medical services and academic research.
  • Legal obligations from government agencies.

6. Data Storage and Security

Data is stored on Google Firebase Cloud Infrastructure.

  • Access restricted to authorized personnel.
  • Password Security: User passwords are secured using hashing algorithms.
  • Authentication systems to verify user identity.
  • Note: General data stored in the database is not encrypted at rest.

7. Data Retention Period

  • 5 years minimum per Hospital Act B.E. 2541.
  • Up to 10 years from last treatment date.
  • Longer if required by legal obligations or disputes.

8. Disclosure or Transfer of Personal Data

  • With written consent.
  • Required by law or government order.
  • Academic research (subject to ethics approval and anonymization).

9. Human Research Ethics

  • Any use of data for research purposes requires approval from the Human Research Ethics Committee.
  • Data must be anonymized.
  • Must not adversely affect rights or dignity.

10. Rights of the Data Subject

  • Right to access.
  • Right to correction.
  • Right to deletion.
  • Right to object (in certain cases).
  • Right to withdraw consent.

To exercise these rights, contact details are listed in Section 11.

11. Contact Information

Data Controller: Thammasat University Hospital

Email: sw.tse68@gmail.com

12. Policy Updates

The Hospital may update this policy without prior notice. Changes will take effect upon publication.
